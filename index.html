Share your algorithm with biologists everywhere — no GUI development required.

We get it. You’ve got a killer method for analyzing microscopy data and would love to get it in the hands of more biologists, but you’ve got better things to do than build user interfaces or learn Java.

But by building your tool as a napari plugin, you don’t have to worry about any of that. Just build your tool as a python package, add a few lines of code, register it with napari.dev, et voila, its ready for biologists everywhere to use.

Napari is a new, Python-based, open source tool for visualizing and annotating 3D, time-series, and multi-channel microscopy data. Led by a team of microscopy experts with deep expertise in large imaging datasets and machine learning, napari is quickly becoming the preferred tool for visualizing and exploring microscopy data. And soon, napari will support analysis plugins, letting you easily package and share your method for segmentation, denoising, tracking, detection and more.

Come join us. Be a part of the community of data scientists, bioimage analysts, imaging scientists and biologists lowering barriers to access the latest methods in analyzing microscopy data.

Python first

You shouldn’t have to learn Java just to make your tool easy for biologists to use. napari plugins are written in Python, letting you use the machine learning and scientific computing frameworks you love, like PyTorch, Tensorflow, Dask, and more.
